About

I am a self-taught web developer with big goals to transition to tech after working in healthcare for the last decade.

Ever since I was a kid, I've enjoyed tech. From the 5-year-old girl learning to type with Mavis Beacon, to the college freshman getting her first campus job in the computer lab, it's always been a comfortable space for me. After losing my job as a Social Services manager due to COVID-19, I had the unexpected gift of time and reflection. I began to realize the skills on my resume, and the things I was most proud of in my past experience, were not exclusive to healthcare. I was drawn to data (and data visualization). I was happiest when I was at my desk creating functions in spreadsheets and preparing reports in graphically meaningful ways. That little girl who so loved Mavis Beacon just wanted to keep exploring what this computer could do. And for the first time, doing that felt like a smart career move, too.

I've spent the last few months learning web development (HTML, CSS, and JavaScript so far) and working on my own projects. I am a natural critical thinker and organizer, and have been enjoying the learning process immensely. I am open to freelance projects in the languages I've worked with so far, and look forward to continuing my studies to join the field in an official capacity very soon.
